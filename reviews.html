An incredible tale of two cities. The most promising entry on the 30 Minute Film Festival. For audiences who are acquainted with the filmmakers' previous works, this film does not disappoint - it's dynamic and authentic, just like the previous works.

New Dhabi/Abu York is one of the most vibrant documentaries produced by up-and-coming creators. There is depth, there is color, there is movement!

If you are looking for a consistent narrative, this might not be it. However, the documentary surprises with its poignant portrayal of city life. Something we experience every day, but represented through a different angle. A notable work for both filmmakers.

The way we are deeply connected to cities tells something about humans. Is it our drive for connection? For comfort? Our desire to be modern? Aruzhan Zhakaibekova and Aigerim Zhusubalieva offer an interesting take on urban life, an indispensable attribute of human life today.
