New Dhabi or Abu York: What stories do our cities tell?

We live, we work, we create, and we love in cities. We also live to create the cities that we love. New York and Abu Dhabi - two vibrant hubs for trade, connection, and creativity. This documentary film aims to draw incredible parallels between these two nodes on a world map.

The film explores the themes of urban life, youth, creativity, and human connection. Immersing the audience into the cityscapes, sounds, and colors of the cities, New Dhabi/Abu York is a kaleidoscope of memories and stories. Despite distance, humans enjoy music, laugh, and show kindness in the same way. Perhaps we are heading towards a new future of global cities, where New Dhabi and Abu York are a possibility.

Premiered at the 30 Minute Film Festival in Cannes in 2022, the film is scheduled to release to a wider audience on February 17, 2022.

About the Filmmakers

Aigerim Zhusubalieva

As a world-renowned documentary filmmaker, Aigerim Zhusubalieva has greatly impacted the field. Zhusubalieva's films are known for distinct style, immersive storytelling, and empowering messages. Her first work titled Becoming (2017) was set in the filmmaker's home country, Kyrgyzstan. Becoming explored the stories of women trailblazers and received immense amount of accolade. Zhusubalieva then collaborated extensively with other filmmakers throughout the world, producing The Bridge (2018), The Stories of Berlin (2019), The Face of Resilience (2020), and Where Did They Go? (2021). Currently she is a director of the Abu Dhabi Arts and Film Center, as well as the International Documentary Film Foundation.

Aruzhan Zhakaibekova

Aruzhan Zhakaibekova is an independent filmmaker from Kazakhstan. Zhakaibekova's works focus on issues of gender, belonging, and public spaces. In 2015, the filmmaker released her first work titled Growing Up, which gained great critical acclaim as a coming-of-age film. Her other notable works include The Paths We Take (2018), Silence (2020), and A Parachute (2021). Aside from filmmaking, Zhakaibekova is involved in a feminist activist group in her home town, Astana, Kazakhstan.
